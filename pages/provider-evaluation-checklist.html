Directory MVP checklist

Provider evaluation checklist for restaurant refrigeration decisions

This checklist shows what a future directory entry would need to prove before it could become a live listing, featured placement, sponsorship, or buyer-facing recommendation.

Current status: no live directory ranking, provider recommendation, dispatch, payment, or lead collection exists.
Every listed proof item remains inactive until it is supplied and approved by a real provider.

Minimum proof before a provider can be treated as directory-ready

Proof AreaAcceptable EvidenceBlocked Shortcut
Business identityProvider-approved legal or trade name and public service-area truth.Invented local office, copied brand identity, or vague "near me" framing.
Commercial refrigeration scopeClear work categories for walk-ins, reach-ins, prep tables, freezers, or ice machines.Residential-only appliance copy stretched into commercial claims.
Emergency availabilityProvider-approved after-hours policy, diagnostic fee language, and response expectations.Unverified fast-response or saved-inventory promises.

Privacy and routingApproved visitor disclosure before any phone, form, booking, or tracking rail exists.Hidden lead resale, unapproved call tracking, or private-data collection.

Evaluation view by repair category

Coolers and freezers

  • Temperature drift, door seal, airflow, evaporator, compressor, and thaw-risk content is specific.
  • Provider proof explains what can be diagnosed remotely and what requires an onsite inspection.
  • No food-safety, inventory, or arrival-time promise is made without authority.

Ice machines

  • Production, filtration, cleaning, drainage, sanitation, and model questions are covered.
  • Provider proof names real scope instead of implying every brand is supported.
  • No sanitation outcome or same-day repair claim is active.

Emergency and maintenance

  • Emergency pages organize operator information without pretending to dispatch work.
  • Maintenance pages explain recurring-service value without inventing a plan or price.
  • Conversion stays off until the future monetization gate is passed.
